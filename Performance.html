1.Wider Wheels and Tires:

improved road-holding turn-in precision,and braking.Larger tires improves handling and cornering,due to wider tread faces and stiffer sidewalls.Wider tires may decrease braking distances on dry pavement.Wider tires may also increase acceleration,especiallyin very powerful vehicles such as muscle cars.

2.High performance shocks/springs/cil-over springs:

Advantages:
Tires stay in contact with the road on bumps and at higher speeds.
Trade-offs:
Ride is stiffer, steering feels rougher.
The B12 Pro-Kit offered by spring-maker Eibach and shock-maker Bilstein
combines springs and shocks that will slightly lower your car's ride height
and are tuned to work together.

3.Larger Anti-roll bars:

Advantages:
Car stays level with the road, optimizing tire contact patch.
Trade-offs:
Bumps on one side are transferred to the other side, which makes for a rougher ride.



4.Adjustable top strut mounts braces

Advantages:
Greater alignment range allows fine-tuning for better.
Trade-offs:
More noise from the road comes through to the car body. Along with wider tires and lowered springs, the best way to fine-tune your alignment for cornering is to add adjustable top strut mounts, also called camber plates, in place of your front suspension's fixed rubber mounts.

5.Larger diameter catalyst-back exhaust:

advantages:
More efficient high-speed engine operation.
Trade-offs:

6.Stiffer motor mounts:

Advantages:
Better engine response and handling precision.
trade-offs:
More engine noice and driveline vibration.
Nissan struggled with driveline mounts when it first introduced the 2003 350Z, trying
to balance vibration versus unwanted engine movement.

7.Performance Seat:

Advantage:
Greater body support for better steering and pedal control.
Trade-offs:
Bumps that jiggle your insides.
We've found that firm racing-style seats are surprisingly comfortable if they support you
in the right places, but they also have to hold you in place as your car subjects you
to g-forces, which means the ride could get a bit uncomfortable.

8.High-Temperature brake pads:

Advantage:
Less fade during heavy brake use and faster decleration.
Trade-offs:
Poorer brake performance when cold or wet,and squealing noise.
Racing brake pad maker Carbotech offers a high-performance version called the 1521 for
street cars.Carbotech also makes a varied array of race and autocross high-temperature
pads, but warns of excess noise when they're used on the street.

9.Less restrictive cold-air intake:

Advantage:
More efficient engine operation,especially at higher speeds.
trade-offs:
Intake noise and maintenance need increase.
While the factory air intakes on most cars are designed to keep the engine clean and
free-breathing in all conditions, opening up the path with a foam filter that doesn't have a
stock bug-catcher airbox lets cooler ambient air help the engine
produce slightly more power.

10.Stainless-steel braided brake lines:

Advantage:
Quicker brake responce,less chance of ruptured line.
Trade-offs:
It could be pricey but is mighty effective.
By using stainless-steel braided brake lines
more of the pressure you put on the pedal is transferred to the brake caliper than with a rubber brake line.
